Personal Projects

Cryptotrage

Back when cryptocurrency markets were less efficient and more volatile, prices varied wildly across exchanges. I have been trading currencies since 2013 and in 2015, I built a trading bot to find inefficiencies and forecast low-risk trades. Using statiscial arbitrage techniques (graph theory algorithms), I was able to profit from mispricings. Nonetheless, this was scary to run autonomously for a 19 year-old with little liquidity, especially as many assets were needed to be distributed across exchanges at the same time. This notion of conducting arbitrage in cryptocurrency circles is outdated due to increased market actors, nonzero transaction times and fees.

More recently, I acted as a Marker Marker in several low volume cryptocurrency markets. I was able to consistently find bid-ask spreads representing up to ~20% of market price. Market Making ended up being orders of magntiude more profitable than conducting stat arb.

Decentralized ETH Exchange

I created a Ethereum distributed application (dApp) to allow for decentralized Ethereum derivative trading with a friend at MIT. Specifically, we created a manual order execution exchange for Ethereum Options. Options are trustlessly and securely held in smart contracts on the Ethereum Blockchain, meaning there is no reliance on a third party. We use Oraclize to retrieve provably secure price data, and IPFS to reduce blockchain storage in a cryptographically secure way.
